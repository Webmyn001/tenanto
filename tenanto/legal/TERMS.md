# Tenanto — User Terms of Service

**Version: 2026-04**

By creating an account on Tenanto you agree to these terms. They are summarised in plain language; the version dated above is the binding text.

## 1. Who you must be

- 18 years or older, or registered with the consent of a parent/guardian
- A genuine person (not a bot, not impersonating someone else)
- Legally able to rent property in Nigeria

## 2. Honesty

- Documents you upload (ID, school email, NIN) must be your own and current
- You must not create multiple accounts, share your account, or sell access
- Reviews you write must reflect your actual experience

## 3. Payments

- All rent payments must go through the Tenanto platform
- We hold rent in escrow and release it to the landlord only after you confirm move-in (or after the 7-day auto-release window if you don't open a dispute)
- Disputes must be opened within 7 days of move-in
- Inspection fees are refundable if a landlord doesn't show up; otherwise they credit toward your rent

## 4. Anti-bypass

- You will not solicit or accept off-platform payment from a landlord
- You will not share phone numbers, email addresses, or external links to bypass our chat
- Repeated bypass attempts may result in account suspension

## 5. Data

- We collect what's needed to verify your identity and process your rental: name, NIN, school email, ID documents, payment data
- We encrypt sensitive identifiers (e.g. NIN) at rest
- We do not sell your data
- You can request deletion of your data subject to NDPR retention requirements (audit logs are kept for 1 year minimum)

## 6. Liability

- Tenanto facilitates rentals; we are not the landlord, and we are not party to the lease
- We do our best to verify landlords and listings but make no warranty about property condition
- Our liability is limited to the platform fees you have paid

## 7. Termination

- You can close your account at any time from settings
- We can suspend or close your account if you violate these terms

## 8. Changes

- If we change these terms materially, you'll be asked to re-accept on your next login

## 9. Disputes

- Disputes between you and a landlord go through our internal resolution process first
- If unresolved, disputes are subject to the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria
